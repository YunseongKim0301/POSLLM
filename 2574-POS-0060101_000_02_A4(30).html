The EGR shall be designed for low sulphur distillate fuels (max. sulphur contents 0.1% m/m) and LNG excluding HFO. The main engine shall be delivered with a NOx technical file and EIAPP certificate in compliance with Tier III NOx emissions levels as per IMO Rules and Regulations.

The EGR installation, material and testing shall be as per Designer’s standard and recommendations.

The exhaust gas emissions shall be Tier II levels by by-passing the EGR outside ECAs.

Onboard verification of NOx emission from the engine shall be carried out by means of engine parameter check method, if modification of the engine components affecting NOx emissions is made on board.

The provision (blind flange) for future measuring of the NOx emissions shall be fitted at each exhaust gas system, and the measuring equipment shall be provided by the Owner, if required.

If NOx measurement device is installed according to supplier’s standard, it shall be designed and tested according to NOx technical code and MEPC 291(71) and described in NOx technical file.

If NOx measurement device is not installed, NOx spot check shall be carried out during sea & gas trial by the supplier according to NOx technical code and MEPC 291(71).

In case that substantial modification is made after the shop test, complete NOx measurement shall be carried out by the Supplier with the Supplier’s own expense.

A copy of technical files shall be submitted to the Buyer for reference within one (1) month after shop test.

Five (5) copies of final technical file including the original copy shall be submitted to the Buyer at the engine delivery.

4.2 GI control system

The GI control system shall include a main operating panel (MOP), which shall have the facilities for manual

The equipment for GI control system shall consist of the multipurpose controller units and the gas cylinder safety

- Second Fuel Cyliner Safety Unit (SCSU), to perform the overall control of the start, stop and purging sequence

and the sealing oil system

- Second Fuel Auxiliary Control Unit (SACU) & Second Fuel Plant Control Unit (SPCU), to control, gas supply

system, inert gas system and ventilation fan system

- Cylinder control units (CCU), to control the electronically gas injection (ELGI) valves

- Second Fuel Plant Safety Unit (SPSU), to monitor the FG system and to control the FG shutdown

- Gas cylinder safety system units (GCSU), to monitor the cylinders in order to secure the FG injection by using the parameters such as accumulator pressure drop, pilot oil injection and cylinder pressure.

The GI control system shall be a single system without manual back-up control, however the following equipment shall be made with redundancy to secure that a single fault will not cause FG stop :

- Dual communication network between the control units

- Mechanical redundancy of the sealing oil pumps with automatic change over

- Separate installation of the essential sensor for the safe FG operation

The GI control system related to mainly hardware shall be divided into the following sub-system :

- Plant control system

- Fuel control system

- Safety system

4.3 Plant control system

The plant control system shall handle the switch between the two stable modes :

- FO only mode

- DF mode

The plant control system shall unify the control of FG equipment and auxiliary systems during start, stop, blow out and purging in each mode.

In case of failures in the normal sequence, the mode shall be returned to the FO only mode i.e. FG injection shall be stopped.

Manual mode selection between the FO only mode and the DF mode shall be possible when running the engines at a load above 10% of ME SMCR.

Change from FO to FG operation and vice versa shall be controlled by a ramp function, which gradually decrease /increase the FO amount in order to avoid disturbances in the speed control. However a FG shutdown command from the safety system shall overrule the ramp function so that pilot oil index is set to unlimited without delay.

The ventilation between the outer and inner walls shall be always in operation when there is gas in the supply line and manual purging of the FG pipes and FG accumulators with inert gas shall be possible when DF running is stopped.

4.4 Fuel control system

The fuel control system shall determine the FG index and the pilot oil index based on fuel index commend from the ECU with GI control function.

The fuel control system shall be operated in the following three different modes, which can be selected from the ME main operating panel (MOP) :

- Gas mode

- Diesel mode

- SDF (Specific Dual Fuel) mode

In gas only mode, the start of the FGS for ME-GI shall be done manually by operator. The stop of the FGS for ME GI shall be automatically controlled without manual intervention during unmanned operation.

4.16 Safety system

The safety system shall monitor the FG equipment, vital auxiliary function, engine shut down signal from existing safety system and the cylinders for being in a condition allowing FG injection. If a critical failure is detected or engine shutdown is activated, the safety system shall release a FG shutdown sequence.

During the DF mode operation, the mode shall be automatically changed to the FO only mode in the following abnormal conditions :

- Gas concentration at (1*) of LEL (Lower Explosion Limit) extracted from the double-wall gas pipe in the outer side.

(1*) Gas concentration level shall be designed in accordance with the requirement of Classification Society.

- Sealing oil, low-low pressure ME cylinder pressure monitoring system

- Air flow in the double-wall gas outer pipe, non-flow

- Gas in accumulator, too low pressure drop during gas injection

- Failures in the control system of the dual fuel mode operation

- Others : According to the manufacturer’s recommendations

When the FG shutdown is activated, the FG master valve shall be closed and the gas pipes shall be blown out and purged by inert gas (nitrogen) for gas-freeing the complete gas supply system.

The safety system shall be also provided and interfaced with the fixed gas detection system to check the methane gas concentration of the air in the double-wall gas pipes.
