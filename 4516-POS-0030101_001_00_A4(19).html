2.1.2 Cover type

The hatch covers for No.1-20 hatches shall be of non-weathertight pontoon type with labyrinth construction on hatch coaming and max. 50mm gap at longitudinal panel joints subject to the approval of the Classification Society.

Each hatch cover shall be of open web construction and the top of hatch covers shall be flat in accordance with manufacturer`s design.

The hatch covers shall be fabricated mainly with higher tensile steel and partly mild steel in accordance with the manufacturer's standards.

The total stack load on hatch cover and container stanchion shall be 100 metric tons (MT) for 20ft and 200 metric tons (MT) for 40ft/45ft containers and 250 MT for mixed loading. (Based on loading 40ft container on 3 tiers of 20ft containers with 24MT)

The permissible stack weight, load distribution, number of tiers in container stack and GM for container securing shall be decided in accordance with the requirements of the Classification Society by the cargo securing manual supplier.

2.1.3 Arrangement of container and container fittings

(1) The longitudinal distance between two(2) adjacent 20ft containers on hatch cover shall be about 76 mm.

(2) The covers shall be designed to allow any combination of empty or full stacks of containers.

(3) The transverse clearance between the containers on hatch cover/deck shall be 37mm in general.

The transverse spacing at longitudinal panel joint area of hatch covers shall be decided to allow for the loading/unloading of the containers into/from the cargo holds with the same number of containers on respective hatch cover panel when the nearby hatch cover panel is closed.

(4) The containers loaded on above the mooring deck shall be supported by partial deck and/or

container stanchions.

2.1.4 Arrangement of container and container fittings

(1) The transverse spacing between the containers in cargo holds shall be about 82 mm.

(2) Height of fixed container fittings including hatch cover lifting socket for 20ft and 40ft spreaders on cover is about 110 mm. (Exact height shall be confirmed by the Supplier considering hatch cover deformation and mixed loading)

(3) The hatch cover top plating shall be reinforced by doubling plate (min. thickness 6 mm subject to class approval) in way of lifting sockets.

(4) Protrusion of about 40mm by entry cell guides above hatch coaming top shall be considered in the hatch cover design (hatch covers not to interfere with entry guides), however the exact protrusion shall be informed by the Buyer later on.

(5) The weight distribution shall be confirmed by the supplier.

(6) The container which are loaded at the outermost sides on deck shall be supported by the hatch

covers and container stanchions in general.

The containers which are loaded at the outermost sides on deck shall be 40ft containers only.

(7) Bay 26 and 86 is not applied hatch cover.

(8) Fully automatic twistlocks (except those under first tier on hatch covers and container stanchions) shall be used for interconnections between the tiers of containers. Bottom twistlocks shall be used under first tier.

(9) The container securing arrangement on deck shall be provided in compliance with Amendments to the CODE OF SAFE PRACTICE FOR CARGO STOWAGE AND SECURING (CSS CODE, MSC.1/Circ.1352) Annex 14.

2.1.6 Cover structure and design load

(1) Structure

Consideration shall be given to the design for easy maintenance and survey.

The lower edge of the transverse skirt plate shall be reinforced according to the Supplier's standard with additional reinforcement in way of guides.

(2) Cover top : Flat top type

(3) Hatch cover to have max. 800 mm height from hatch coaming top when closed, and girder including stacking fitting not to exceed 950 mm.

(4) Maximum deflection of the cover shall be about 45 mm at mid span. (static/dynamic condition according to the Rule requirements)

The hatch cover movement due to deformation of hatch coaming shall be considered

-Exact hatch coaming movement value will be confirmed later on

The maximum lifting weight of one(1) panel shall not exceed about 40 metric tons including the fixed lashing fittings and loose fittings (twist locks) on the hatch cover.

(5) Design load

According to the requirement of Rule and Regulation and the Classification Society

(6) Local reinforcement

Necessary arrangement including local reinforcements, positions, etc., for container base sockets, lifting sockets, lashing eyes, etc., shall be included on the approval drawings.

The arrangement drawing to state the building tolerances, which shall be guaranteed by the Supplier.

The tolerance of the coamings shall be also stated for the Buyer to keep.

The Supplier shall state the building tolerances separately on the tolerance table.

(The building tolerances not shall be indicated on the approval drawing).

(7) The design condition of hatch covers shall be max. 3o heel and 2o trim of the Vessel, or according to the requirements of Classification Society.

Trim and heel may be simultaneous.

(8) IMO UR S21 and UR S35 of latest version shall be reflected.

And IACS UR C7 also should be reflected for material and certificate.

2.1.7 Cover cleating

All hatch covers shall be secured to the top plates of the hatch coaming by manual anti-lifting device as per the Class rule and the manufacturer's standard, the number of which shall be minimized.

2.1.8 Hatch cover handling

(1) The hatch covers shall be handled non-sequentially by the shore facility.

(2) The lifting sockets for hatch cover handling by container spreaders for 20ft and/or 40ft containers shall be fitted on each hatch cover panel, but the container base sockets on hatch covers may be commonly used for hatch cover handling.

(3) The hatch cover top plating shall be reinforced by doubling plate in way of lifting sockets.

(4) The lifting sockets shall be placed symmetrically at the center of gravity of hatch cover to achieve good weight balance.

After hatch cover design is finished, the supplier should submit officially confirmation letter of weight balance calculation.

(5) The lifting holes on the stacking bar of hatch cover top shall be utilized for lifting the hatch covers with slings by the shore facility.

(6) 20ft/40ft container spreader shall be placed symmetrically at center gravity of hatch cover.

2.1.9 Stacking

(1) The stacking fittings shall be provided on the hatch covers for stowing the hatch cover on another hatch cover (total four(4) hatch covers), i.e., three(3) hatch covers shall be stowed on

one(1) other side cover.

When stacking, sufficient height considering bottom twistlock and midlock shall be provided between each stacked panels.

The twist locks shall be rest on the covers and/or container base sockets during the stacking.

(2) Five(5) panels stacking plan shall be submitted to the Buyer for construction stage.

(3) The stacking of the hatch covers shall be made in a symmetrical pattern as far as practicable.

(4) Stacking operation for on-board and on-shore shall be considered.

(total four(4) hatches at random).

2.1.10 Bearing pads

(1) In closed position, the hatch covers shall rest on support bearing pads which introduce the vertical force into hatch coaming. The pads shall be externally located, and specially designed for the movement of hatch covers with regard to the hatch coaming deformation as well as the additional forces due to lashing bridge and/or lashing fittings.

(2)The bearing pad shall be provided with manufacturer's standard spares and additional slanted shims according to the manufacturer's standards as follows :

Type

Flexible/non-sliding replaceable

Material

Laminated rubber

Location

Under hatch cover

(3) The size of pads shall be unified and minimized as far as practicable.

(4) The friction coefficient used between the bearing surfaces for dimensioning the hatch coaming steel structure shall be within the requirement of the Classification Society.

(5) The Supplier shall be responsible for and to guarantee the perfect function of the pads.

(6) The concerned Regulations and Requirements of the Classification Society shall be applied for the horizontal forces in longitudinal and transverse directions.

(7) Necessary calculation, analysis, etc., including fatigue calculation in the area of hatch cover supports shall be carried out by the Supplier to ensure the correct design of pads and shall be submitted to the Buyer for approval.

(8) The submission of the analyses does neither dispense the Supplier from his global responsibility for the design, nor from the unlimited responsibility for the contractual guarantee.

(9) The larger flexible pads shall be provided additionally due to the movement between hatch cover and coaming according to computational analysis conducted by Classification Society.

2.1.11 Stopper

(1) Transverse/longitudinal pin stoppers at forward of hatches and transverse stoppers at aft of hatches shall be provided, which shall be also used to serve the fine entry guide of covers. (The exact location of stoppers shall be clarified / decided during the drawing approval stage.).

The stoppers shall be externally mounted.

(2) Transverse/longitudinal pin stopper shall be shaped to have minimum hull reinforcement (such as con shape).

(3) The design to ensure smooth entering of hatch covers in final Technical Specifications at max. ship's trim of 2.0° and heel of 3.0°, or both simultaneously, or according to the requirements of the Classification Society, if any.

2.1.12 Hatch cover guide

(1) Hatch cover guide shall be provided and integrated into the lashing bridge for guidance of hatch covers.

(2) The covers to smoothly enter at a max. angle of 2.0° trim and 3° heel of the Vessel, or according to the requirements of Classification Society. Trim and heel may be simultaneous.

(3) The Supplier of hatch cover shall be discussed with the Buyer and the Supplier of lashing bridge design for interface checking on hatch coaming.

2.1.13 Marking of hatch cover

(1) The necessary marking including the weight of hatch cover panel, number of hatch cover panel, bay number (for each 20ft and 40ft) and row number shall be provided on hatch covers, which shall be informed by the Buyer later on.(But, refer to Yangming standard)

The marking shall be done by means of continuous welding bead with painting.

The standard drawing for marking will be provided to the Supplier.

Row numbers on hatch cover top plate shall be located 50mm far away to from fore & aft edge of the top plate. All marking position shall not be located at lifting socket and container socket position.

(2) Weight of hatch covers shall be intermittently welded and painted on hatch cover top.

(3) The container lashing fittings(sockets, eyes), hatch cover lifting fittings(sockets, eyes) and stacking fittings on the hatch covers shall be distinguished by reflective paint for ease of identification.

The finished color shall be informed later.

(4) All hatch cover marking drawing should be designed and submitted by real scale for checking interference between hatch cover fitting on top plate and marking.

2.1.14 Other equipment on hatch cover

(1) Ventilation flap

Information will be informed later.

(2) Container fixed fittings

Single socket on side most row (Sea side) of side panel shall be strengthening type.

(3) Water spray system

Shall not be provided.

(4) Connection bracket between cutter bar and container socket are to be checked deeply in order to easy welding, namely narrow gap does not provided between gutter bar and container socket.

(5) Pin stopper design should be minimized.

(2) All necessary calculations, analysis, etc., required by the Classification Society as well as the Supplier's standard shall be submitted free of charge by the Supplier before the commencement of hatch cover fabrication.
