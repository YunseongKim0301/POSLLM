2. Technical Specification

2.1 Design criteria

2.1.1 General

The Vessel shall be equipped with a cargo handling system for loading, transportation and discharging the liquefied natural gas (hereinafter called the “LNG”).

The cargo containment and handling system shall be designed to load the LNG in fourteen (14) hours and discharge the LNG in fourteen (14) hours, and to carry the LNG in four (4) tanks at low temperature of -163oC.

The cargo containment system shall be of membrane type in accordance with the design of GTT and the requirements of the Classification Society, the Rules and Regulations.

Inert gas/dry air system shall be provided for the following purposes:

- To supply inert gas into the cargo tanks during gas purging within 22 hours.

- To supply inert gas into the cargo tanks during air purging within 22 hours.

- To supply dry air into the cargo tanks during aeration within 22 hours.

- To supply dry air for drying the cargo tanks.

-To supply inert gas for inerting the cargo piping via blind flange valve or spool piece.

-To supply dry air to pipe duct and ballast tank aeration via spool piece.

2.2 System design

One (1) inert gas generating plant using the LSMGO(DMA ISO 8217)as fuel shall be provided in ER to produce the inert gas and dry air.

The capacity of the plant shall be 16,000 Nm3/h with a discharge pressure of 0.25 bar g at inert gas generating mode and the system shall consists of the following equipment:

- One (1) burner/scrubber unit

- One (1) FO supply unit including fuel pump, electric motor, filter, etc.

- Two (2) combustion air blowers (each 50% of system capacity)

- One (1) cooling unit

- One (1) dryer unit with electric and steam heaters for regeneration

- Two (2) fixed oxygen analyzers

- Integral valves

- Control and alarm panels

The construction and performance shall be in accordance with supplier’s standard in compliance with the latest requirements of Classification Societies and Regulatory bodies.

The design of the plant such as construction, materials, corrosion protections, safety devices, etc. shall be suitable for marine service and also to meet the design guidance for “Inert Gas System” of IMO.

The materials contacted with humid inert gas or sea water shall be corrosion resistant stainless steel or special material. Blowers shall be suitably protected to prevent corrosion.

2.3 General description of the equipment

2.3.1 Scrubber/Burner unit

The stoichiometric inert gas shall be produced by the combustion of oil with air in the combustion chamber.

The combustion chamber of the inert gas generator shall be of horizontal or vertical type with suitable drainage system so as to avoid potential pollution during start up and in the event of flame failure.

The gas from the combustion chamber shall pass through an integral scrubbing section for washing out Sulphur oxide and cooling the gases with the effluent from the scrubbing section being fed overboard through a water seal or equivalent means.

The demister for dehumidification of wet inert gas shall be provided at the end of scrubbing/cooling section.

If the vertical type scrubber unit shall be applied, a scrubber drain tank connected scrubber drain line

shall be provided for preventing an oil contamination at flame failure mode in accordance with

supplier’s recommendation.

The interior steel surface of the scrubber/burner unit shall be of high temperature resistant stainless steel or other suitable material according to the supplier’s standards.

The scrubber/burner unit to be designed to prevent oil spill from fuel nozzle through the overboard discharge pipe whenever system shutdown or burner failure, with constructive feature or by using anti-pollution arrangement.

Arrangement shall be provided to stop sea water supply pump and divert from overboard to bilge holding tank to prevent oil pollution due to misfire or bad combustion.

Adequate openings and sight glasses shall be provided in the shell for inspection, cleaning and observational purposes. The sight glasses to be reinforced to withstand impact and be of a heat resisting type. This may be achieved by the use of double glazing.

The design to be such that under normal conditions of trim and list the scrubber efficiency will not fall by more than 3 %, nor will the temperature rise at the gas outlet exceed the designed gas outlet temperature by more than 3℃.

The dedicated inert gas scrubber pump shall not be provided, but No.2 ballast pump shall be used as

inert gas scrubber pump.

The ballast pump shall be controlled by the control panel of inert gas system when the mode

selector switch on the pump starter is positioned at the scrubber cooling mode.

The interlock system between ballast pump and IGG shall be provided in order to stop the SW

supplying from ballast pump for cooling/washing of inert gas.

Added and extend the FO inlet connection for FO leak protection.

2.3.2 Combustion air blower

The combustion air blowers shall be of root type or multi stage centrifugal type and driven by a

single-speed electric motor. The materials of the combustion air blowers shall be in accordance with

the supplier’s standards.

Two (2) sets of blowers, each capable of delivering 50% of the total system shall be provided.

In normal condition, two (2) combustion air blowers to be operated simultaneously, but it shall also be possible to operate the plant with one (1) blower (50% of the total system capacity) only.

Sufficient openings in the casing shall be provided to permit inspection.

The blower pressure/volume characteristics to be matched to the maximum system requirements.

The prime mover (electric motor) for blowers to be of sufficient power to ensure that it will not be overloaded under all possible operating conditions of the blower, especially at low back pressure condition (i.e., gas freeing mode, air venting, etc.).

Arrangements shall be provided to maintain the windings in a dry condition during the inoperative period.

The combustion air blowers shall complete with thermistor protection in their winding.

Natural frequencies of the combustion air blowers with resilient mountings to be 10 percent away from main excitation frequencies by main engine and propeller at NCR and MCR.

Main engine & propeller data are as follows:

- Main engine : MAN B&W 5G70ME-C10.5-GI (Derated) x 2 sets

- Propulsion power : 11,640 kW x approx. 67.4 rpm, each for MCR

: 9,894 kW x approx. 63.8 rpm for NCR (85% MCR)

- Propeller RPM : approx. 67.4 rpm (MCR) & approx. 63.8 rpm (NCR)

- Propeller blade : 4 (to be decided later)

Calculation results of natural frequencies for longitudinal, transverse, vertical, rolling, pitching, and yawing modes to be submitted to the Shipyard with approval drawings.

Vibration measurement results of shop test to be submitted to the Shipyard with shop test report.

Impeller balancing data including the amount of eccentricity and unbalanced mass shall be submitted to

the Shipyard with shop test report.

The combustion air blowers shall be flexibly mounted (Resilient rubber mounts).

2.3.3 Cooling unit

The cooling unit shall consist of a refrigerant gas compressor, fresh water-cooled condenser and inert gas cooler, necessary control unit, valves, etc., and shall be built as a common unit with control equipment for automatic operation.

R-407C shall be used for refrigerant of gas compressor for environmental consideration. And a detector to be provided for detection of refrigerant(R-407C) leakage around cooling unit and shall be monitored with alarm in IAS.

Cooling water to condenser in refrigeration unit and cooler in dryer unit shall be supplied by a cooling fresh water pump of central fresh water cooling system.

2.3.4 Dryer unit

Dryer unit shall be equipped with electric and steam heaters for regeneration.

Cool down the gas from the cooling unit to achieve a dew point of -45oC at atmospheric pressure

Two (2) absorption vessels shall consist of drying towers filled with activated alumina and regenerated automatically by air heater.

Change-over from drying operation to regeneration shall take place in accordance with the supplier’s standard or every eight hours as option.

Facilities to be provided on the dryer unit to allow regeneration in the absence of a supply of steam in order to allow the unit to produce dry air in the event of the Vessel being laid up.

Cooling water to dryer unit cooler shall be supplied by cooling fresh water pump of central fresh water cooling system (temperature of 36℃).

It shall be possible to run or regenerate the dryers without running the blowers.

2.3.5 Fixed oxygen analyzer

One (1) – working, one (1) – stand-by fixed oxygen analyzer shall be fitted on the inert gas main to monitor the oxygen content during operation, and alarm shall be provided when the oxygen content exceeds set point.

The type of oxygen analyzer shall be in accordance with the supplier’s standard.

2.3.6 Inert gas/vent piping

The inert gas treated and stabilized by the inert gas system to be distributed into the cargo tanks through inert gas main line and cargo liquid main line via non‑return valves, isolating valve and spool piece.

The inert gas shall be supplied from ER to the cargo tanks through the inert gas main line and the cargo liquid main line.

The gas mixtures from cargo tanks to be discharged to the No.1 cargo tank vent riser through the cargo vapor line.

The piping shall be arranged such that the inert gas may be automatically vented to the atmosphere in case of high dew point or excessive oxygen content.

The deck isolating non-return valve shall have external indication of its position and a means of operating the valve.

The inert gas main and one (1) of the ballast discharge main lines shall be cross-connected with shut-

off valve and a portable spool piece so that fresh air can be blown into the double bottom spaces of

the WB tanks by using the inert gas blower.

An air branch line with spool piece and shut off valve on the inert gas main for ventilation of pipe

duct shall be provided. A connection with blind flange shall be provided on the vent duct for pipe

duct.

2.3.7 Control and alarm system

The control and alarm system for the inert gas system shall be in accordance with the supplier's

standards, the requirements of the Classification Society, the Rules and Regulations.

Main control panel with system functional mimic diagram fitted with necessary controllers and

instruments shall be provided in ER. The system shall be started/stopped locally at main control

panel and remotely monitored via the VDUs of IAS in CCR.

The plant shall, after starting, be controlled automatically by the control system with alarm in the

event of fault conditions in compliance with the requirements of the Classification Society.

The recording of O2 content, dew point, flow, pressure and temperature shall be done through trend

display of IAS.

A fixed oxygen content monitoring sensor shall be provided by Buyer for each IG scrubber and IG

dryer unit for the local atmosphere.

5.1 Protective earthing for electrical equipment and machinery:

Exposed parts of electrical installations and machineries such as metal enclosures, motor’s body, alternator and generator engine’s body and all other equipment which they have current carrying parts and become to live under fault condition are required to have earth connection(s) to hull structure, earthing installations shall be in accordance with rules for applicable Classification Societies, and the Followings are major essential features to be observed by a supplier:

5.1.1 The connection to the hull of earth conductors or equipment enclosure parts, which shall be earthed,

shall be made by corrosion resistant screws or clamps. The earth connection(s) shall be clearly

indicated on the approval drawing(s).

5.1.2 Where the foundation is delivered with equipment, the earth bolt is to be connected to the

foundation via a copper earth conductor by a supplier. The earth connection(s) shall be clearly

indicated on the approval drawing(s).

5.1.3 Where the equipment is delivered without the foundation, the earth bolt which it is easily visible and

accessible shall be fitted, and it shall be clearly indicated on the approval drawing where it is placed. The Buyer recommends its position is to be placed nearby the electrical panel or electrical cable entry.

5.1.4 Earthing screws and clamps shall not be used for other purposes. Suitable star washers and conductor

terminals shall be used, so that a reliable contact is ensured.

5.1.5 The terminal shall be identified by a symbol or legend for protective earthing (PE).

5.1.6 Where the foundation is required for equipment by Buyer, a supplier shall indicate position of

earth bolt recommended on the foot print drawing so that it can be prepared on the foundation by

Buyer.
