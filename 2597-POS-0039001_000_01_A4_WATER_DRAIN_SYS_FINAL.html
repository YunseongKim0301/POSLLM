A) Water drain pump for secondary insulation space (2 set)

2.1 Service condition of drain pump

- Please refer to the 1.3 condition.

2.2 Main design requirements

The construction and material shall be in accordance with the Supplier's standard in compliance with the

latest requirement of class and regulatory bodies, and also this specification.

2.2.1 Particulars

- Q'ty : 2 sets

- Type : Comp. air driven, positive displacement, self-priming double

diaphragm pump

- Capacity : 20 m3/H x 40 MWC

- Compressed air pressure : 5 ~ 8 bar (Service pressure)

8.8 bar (Design pressure)

- Handling liquid : Condensate water and bilge

2.2.2 Material

- Casing : SUS316 BODY

- Valve seats : EPDM or WILFLEX or equivalent

- Valve balls : EPDM or WILFLEX or equivalent

- Diaphragm : EPDM or WILFLEX or equivalent

- The others : The Supplier's standard (Shall be informed)

2.3 Accessories

Following accessories and fitting for each pump shall be included in scope of supply.

2.3.1 Elements for resilient mounting

2.3.2 Flexible connections for pump suction and discharge

2.3.3 Muffler for air exhaust

2.3.4 Air pressure regulator

2.3.5 Air filter

2.3.6 Lubricator with pressure gauge

2.3.7 Needle valve for air inlet

2.3.8 Stainless steel flange for in/outlet connection

2.3.9 Suction compound gauge and discharge pressure gauge with root cooks or valves

2.4 Operating requirement

2.4.1 The drain pump system shall be used for drain water discharge in secondary insulation space.

2.4.2 Drain water should be pumped without vibration.

2.4.3 The pump shall be an air operated, double-diaphragm pump with ball check valves.

2.4.4 Clamp band construction will be provide easy of inspection and maintenance.

2.4.5 Pumping volume shall be controlled by an integral air valve of only one(1) moving part and which

shift without mechanical assistance.

2.4.6 The pump shall be self-priming and have the ability to run dry and dead head.

2.5 General design requirement

Basically, the equipment’s joint design shall be considered about the vibration effect. So, the joint parts (including air line) shall be items that can endure the vibration, like a flexible hose.

6. REMARKS

6.1 Protective earthing for electrical equipment and machinery:

Exposed parts of electrical installations and machineries such as metal enclosures, motor’s body,

alternator and generator engine’s body and all other equipment which they have current carrying parts and become to live under fault condition are required to have earth connection(s) to hull structure.

Earthing installations shall be in accordance with rules for applicable Classification Societies, and the Followings are major essential features to be observed by a Supplier:

6.1.1 The connection to the hull of earth conductors or equipment enclosure parts, which shall be

earthed, shall be made by corrosion resistant screws or clamps. The earth connection(s) shall be

clearly indicated on the approval drawing(s).

6.1.2 Where the foundation is delivered with equipment, the earth bolt is to be connected to the

foundation via a copper earth conductor by a Supplier. The earth connection(s) shall be clearly

indicated on the approval drawing(s).

6.1.3 Where the equipment is delivered without the foundation, the earth bolt which it is easily visible and accessible shall be fitted, and it shall be clearly indicated on the approval drawing where it is

placed. The Buyer recommends its position is to be placed nearby the electrical panel or electrical

cable entry.

6.1.4 Earthing screws and clamps shall not be used for other purposes. Suitable star washers and conductor

terminals shall be used, so that a reliable contact is ensured.

6.1.5 The terminal shall be identified by a symbol or legend for protective earthing (PE).

6.1.6 Where the foundation is required for equipment by Buyer, a Supplier shall indicate position of earth

bolt recommended on the foot print drawing so that it can be prepared on the foundation by Buyer.

6.2 Requirement for Explosion proof equipment (If provided)

6.2.1 The certificates for explosion equipment to be submitted by including the approval drawing and the

following data to be informed as attached files at the design approval stage. And the certificates shall

indicate that the equipment has been tested according to IEC 60079.

6.2.2 The explosion-proof equipment shall be listed in the APPENDIX III with excel format an uploaded to yard on-line explosion proof equipment platform (https://livedata.hanwhaocean.com/) by supplier. If supplier cannot find the excel format, supplier shall request the file format and ask the upload way to the buyer. Maker standard format shall not be used. The inspection and its report of explosion proof equipment shall be carried out and provided during commissioning by the supplier based on the requirement of IEC 60079-14 as part of the commissioning and start up procedures. The inspection report shall include the confirmation of no difference between submitted “explosion proof equipment list” and “actual on-board equipment” by the supplier.

6.2.3 The certificate file shall also be produced electronically with a single line record of explosion proof,

intrinsically or increased safety equipment for the entire vessel.

6.2.4 The equipment shall be certified as a complete assembly ready for installation. If the assembly includes parts that are also certified, the supplier shall provide certificates of conformity for these parts so that documentation is complete and unambiguous.

6.2.5 The markings on the equipment shall correspond to the details of the certificate and the hazardous area

equipment list entry for that equipment.

6.2.6 Each item of Ex-rated equipment shall be provided with a unique identification code.

6.2.7 The explosion proof equipment shall be of Ex-e type or intrinsically safe type(Ex-d type shall be

avoided) as far as practicable. The Ex-type shall be complied with hazardous zone requirement as per

IEC60092-502. Ex) zone0, zone1, etc.

If Ex-d type equipment shall be provided, the grade should be more than Ex-d IIC T4 with certified

cable gland to match the Buyer’s cable type.

If intrinsically safe circuits are provided, the intrinsically safe aspects should be checked by supplier to ensure that sufficient type of IS barriers are selected (refer to IEC60079-25 page62). The result shall be provided if buyer requests.
