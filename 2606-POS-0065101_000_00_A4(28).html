1.5 Vibration

1.5.1 The engine with alternator shall be free from vibration throughout all operation ranges.

The Supplier shall be responsible for any kinds of high vibration of the diesel generator set, exceeding the allowable vibration limit required by the Buyer as follows :

- Engine side : 25.0 mm/s peak in all directions

- Alternator side : 25.0 mm/s peak in all directions

Vibration measurement results shall be submitted to the Buyer with the shop test report.

Measured vibration levels at the shop test and on sea trial shall be below the vibration criteria as stated above.

1.5.2 Natural frequencies for longitudinal, transverse, vertical, rolling, pitching and yawing vibration modes of the diesel generator set shall be calculated to avoid resonance problem with the external excitation frequencies due to the main engine and propeller, and the calculation result shall be submitted to the Buyer with approval drawings.

The natural frequencies of the diesel generator set shall be 10 % away from main excitation frequencies by the main engine and propeller at NCR and MCR.

1.5.3 The torsional vibration calculation for the diesel generator set shall be carried out by the Supplier and shall be submitted to the Classification Society and the Owner for approval.

One(1) copy of the approved calculation sheet shall be submitted to the Buyer.

1.5.4 The vibration levels of the machinery should be in accordance with relevant ISO 10816 series “Evaluation of machinery vibration by measurements non-rotating parts”. If measured vibration levels at shop test and on sea trial (commissioning test) exceed the following vibration limits, the Supplier shall be fully responsible for any kind of high vibration and make necessary improvement to a practical extent under mutual agreement between the Supplier and the Buyer.

(1)ISO 10816-1:2016 : Mechanical vibration - Measurement and evaluation of machine vibration –

Part1:General guidelines

(2)ISO 10816-3:2009/Amend1:2017 Part 3 : Industrial machines with nominal power above 15 kW

and nominal speeds between 120 RPM and 15,000 RPM when measured in situ

(3)ISO 10816-6:1995 Amend1:2015 Part 6 : Reciprocating machines with power ratings

above 100 kW

(4)VDI 3838:2004 : Measurement and evaluation of mechanical vibration of reciprocating piston

- Addition to DIN ISO 10816- 6

1.5.5 The test record of vibration to be measured at the shop test shall be submitted to the Buyer.

1.5.6 Resilient mountings and flexible coupling and connections shall be fitted in all locations which effectively minimizes the transmission of vibration through the structures.

1.5.7 Vibration levels of pumps and rotating machineries shall be in accordance with DNV Vibration class 2011, at the shop and on sea trials.

1.40 Wire reinforced flange joint gasket shall not be used.

The design criteria of pressure and diameter of bursting discs relief shall be informed.

The number and arrangement of rupture disks shall be decided after an explosion analysis. The explosion analysis shall be carried out by the Buyer under the Supplier's supervision.

The exhaust gas fan for GEs shall run long enough to ensure that the entire exhaust gas system is purged with a volume of air equal to at least two times the volume of the system. Confirmation that the fan is running shall be provided by a flow switch, not by sensing that the motor is running. Purging duration of exhaust gas ventilation unit can be adjusted during diesel generator engine commissioning stage.

A manually remote operated butterfly type isolating valve shall be provided by the Supplier between the fan and exhaust pipe. The control system shall inhibit restart of the engine following a purge request unless an effective purge has been completed.

- Engine safety functions, causing an engine shutdown

- Gas system safety functions, causing a gas trip
