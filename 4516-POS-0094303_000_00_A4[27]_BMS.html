1. GENERAL

The IACS UR Requirements of E22, E26, and E27 shall be complied with without any deviation.

Some of those requirements for IACS UR E26 are described below in order to specify the particular information to be provided by each vendor, but they are not limited to the below.

When the Owner provides its cyber resilience policy, it shall be discussed and agreed upon separately.

2. TECHNICAL PARTICULARS

2.1 Technical Requirements for Integrated Ship Equipment Management in Compliance with IACS UR E26

The following information should be required to meet UR E26 requirements and ensure seamless integration and operation of your equipment with other ship systems.

Technical Elements for Integrated Management

To ensure integrated management of ship equipment, including physical and logical interconnectivity, each vendor shall provide the following:

a. Interface Design and Integration Information

·Detailed specifications of physical and software interfaces for your equipment, including:

- Communication protocols (e.g., CAN, Modbus, Ethernet, etc.).

- Data exchange formats and signal types (analog, digital, etc.).

- Real-time performance capabilities (e.g., latency, data throughput).

·Document for the interoperability with other CBS and equipment (e.g., navigation systems, control systems, UTM, Network Switch) to ensure seamless integration.

·Interface control documents (ICDs) or similar detailing data integrity, error detection, and recovery mechanisms.

b. Functional Safety Requirements

·To ensure the functional integrity of CBS when integrating with other equipment, each vendor shall provide the following:

- Redundancy or fail-safe/fail-operational modes in case of failure.

- Synchronization with monitoring and control systems for real-time data sharing.

·Provide details on how each equipment handles potential hazards (e.g., communication errors, data loss) during integration

c. Operational and Maintenance Information

·Outline each equipment's operation and maintenance plan, ensuring compliance with including:

- Procedures for fault detection, reporting, and recovery. (e.g., Recovery Plan, Backup and restore capability, Controlled shutdown, reset, roll-back, and restart)

- User manuals, installation guides, and maintenance instructions.
