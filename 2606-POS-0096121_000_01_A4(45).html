1.GENERAL

- All the POWER PLANTS shall be supplied in accordance with the requirements of the Technical

Specifications except otherwise agreed between Yard and Maker.

- Details not covered in the Technical Specification shall be in accordance with the requirements of Rules&

Regulations in the general terms and drawing approvals.

- All electrical equipment and luminaries shall be of marine quality type approved. In all switchgears,

power board, starters etc., the copper terminal ends of cables greater than 10mm2, must be insulated with

vinyl cap for cable ends greater than 10mm2. All copper terminals must be of seamless type.

- The interrupting capacity of the circuit breakers, which is less than the maximum possible short circuit current

at the point of installation, shall be backed up by a current limiting molded case circuit breaker or a molded

case circuit breaker incorporated with current limiting of high interrupting capacity.

- Distribution systems as switchboards, distribution boards, supply cables of distribution boards shall have

about 10 % spare capacity for possible future modifications and extension, if not otherwise specified in

paragraph in question.

- The supplier shall be responsible for interfacing with IAS(or other control systems) software to carry out all operations defined in the Technical Specification for IAS, to the satisfaction of the buyer. Allowance shall be made, such that no additional cost is involved for changes to development software, as may be found necessary, during commissioning, sea and gas trials of the vessel. This shall include, but not be limited to, sensor set points and range, time delays, alarm blocking groups, text and graphic display, control loop configuration, sequence logic programming etc.

- Low voltage switchgear and control gear assemblies (All sections of LVMSBD, LVCSBD and ESBD) shall be provided in accordance with the recommendation of IEC 61439-1 without the type test of related equipment.

- Terminal number tags shall be provided on each conductor of multicore cables in the main equipment such as HV main switchboards and HV cargo switchboards.

- Cable marking: Cable circuit number tags shall be provided for the external cables of control circuits

inside the main switchboards and cargo switchboards.

- High voltage switchboards shall be provided in accordance with the relevant part of IEC 62271-200 in the requirement of Classification Society.

the switchboard, panels or equipment. The total power factor of the system shall be better than 0.8 lagging

under design conditions.

2.7. MECHANICAL CONSTRUCTION

2.7.1 Type of switchboard

The switchboard shall be of fully partitioned, metal-clad type in arc proof execution based on IEC(High Voltage Metal-Enclosed Switchgear and Controller), dead front and self-standing type, box frame construction with a drip cover over the top and shall have hinged front panels that can be opened without disturbing the meters, pilot lamps, etc. mounted on them.

2.7.2 Protection degree and ambient temperature

The protection degree of out cover to be of IP 32 as per IEC 529.

Anyway, the latest rule is to be applied.

2.7.3 General construction

The switchboard shall be constructed of formed sheet steel structures, and to be capable of withstanding

mechanical, electrical and thermal stresses which may arise from shock or vibration of the ship or short

circuit fault.

All the metal parts composing the mechanical construction shall be protected against corrosion by

effective painting or plating to the exposed surface.

2.7.4 Mechanical construction parts

2.7.4.1 Supporting structures shall be made of formed sheet steel and reinforced so as to ensure

satisfactory strength.

2.7.4.2 Front doors shall be made of formed and fabricated sheet steel.

All front doors shall be made of hinged type or withdrawal type and to be opened up to an angle

of ninety degrees at least.

2.7.4.3 Door stoppers shall be provided for keeping the front doors in an angle of over ninety degrees.

2.7.4.4 Top covers shall be made of sheet steel and they have waves at front and rear.

2.7.4.5 Side covers shall be made of tight sheet steel and firmly fixed to the supporting structure with

screws.

2.7.4.6 Rear door shall be made of formed and fabricated sheet steel or hinged type.

2.7.4.7 Partition plates shall be made of sheet steel between each cubicle to minimize the possibility of

spreading an accidental arc or flame to the neighboring cubicles.

2.7.4.8 Grounded metal barriers shall be installed of sheet steel between major primary sections of each

circuit to minimize the possibility of communicating faults between primary sections.

The Primary sections are the Bus compartment, the High voltage cable termination compartment,

the Power circuit breaker compartment, Etc.

2.7.4.9 Hand rails shall be made of insulating material and fitted horizontally in the front and at the rear

for every panel.

2.7.4.10 Door interlocking devices shall be provided for KEY INTERLOCKING.

2.7.4.11 Pressure Relief Flap shall be made at top position of switchboard, and the compartments are as

follows. If the flap is to be connected to external fresh duct(yard supply), it will be confirmed at

quotation stage how to connect it.

- Circuit breaker compartments

- High voltage cable termination compartments

2.7.4.12 In order to install cables between each panels, if necessary, the cable entrances shall be made at

top position, and the position after installation cable to be fitted with steel plate screwed with bolt.

2.7.4.13 In principal, bottom of switchboard to be opened without steel plate.

But, If it is necessary to install steel plate on construction and rule requirement the location

before contract shall be informed or discussed with Yard.

2.7.4.14 High voltage and low voltage compartments in switchboard shall be clearly distinguished by

grounded metal barriers.

2.7.4.15 Cable entrances for High voltage and Low voltage cables shall be clearly separated.

2.7.4.16 In order to bind cables connected in switchboard, cable hanger and way shall be made at left or

right side of each section inside of switchboard, and specially, the holes on cable hanger shall

be made to vertical direction.

2.7.4.17 Bus-bar shall be proven to short circuit or shall be used a type approved.

2.7.4.18 If requested, mechanical strength calculation report shall be submitted.

2.7.4.19 If the main high-voltage switchboard is divided into two independent and autonomous

installations, a continuous busbar compartment is permissible.

2.7.4.20 All doors shall have hinges locking handle for locking the door in open position. Screw type locks shall not be used.

For color coding, it shall be complied with IEC 60445.

2.8.6 Earthing switches

2.8.6.1 All circuit breakers with incoming and outgoing circuits shall be interlocked with earthing switch,

and when open door, the earthing switch shall be operated.

2.8.6.2 Earthing switches shall be provided with Key interlock and Electrical interlock, and auxiliary

contacts shall be provided for control or alarm monitoring.

2.8.6.3 Interlocking device circuits shall be shown in approval drawing.

2.8.7 Alternator circuit breaker

2.8.7.1 The high voltage main alternators shall be protected by Vacuum or SF6 Circuit Breaker(HCB)

2.8.7.2 The HCB shall be of electrically operated trip free type and controlled by a control switch,

and additionally a manual operating handle shall be provided.

2.8.7.3 The HCB shall have an overcurrent trip device and instantaneous trip and under-voltage trip.

The HCB shall have the capacity enough to interrupt short circuit current.

2.8.7.4 A time delay relay shall be controlled from the overcurrent relay, which causes the tripping of the

alternator circuit breaker if the overload period exceeds the time set. Before this situation is reached, the preferential tripping circuit shall disconnect the non-essential loads.

2.8.7.5 Alternators shall be protected from reverse power by reverse power relay connected to under voltage trip device.

2.8.8 Feeder circuit breaker

2.8.8.1 The high voltage feeder circuits shall be protected by vacuum of SF6 circuit breaker, vacuum contactor with fuse or fused circuit breaker, and low voltage feeder circuit shall be by ACB and/or moulded case circuit breakers (MCCB) with inverse time thermal over current trip, instantaneous magnetic trip and short circuit current interruption features, except the steering gear motor feeders which shall be protected against short circuit only.
