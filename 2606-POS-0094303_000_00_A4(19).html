2. Technical DESCRIPTION

2.1 Operation & controls

2.1.1 Main engine shall be remotely controlled from the wheelhouse (option : bridge wings) and the

ECR.

2.1.2 The ECR manoeuvring shall be electrically actuated by means of the regulating handle.

The wheelhouses manoeuvring shall be electrically actuated by means of the regulating handle

which is also used for the engine telegraph transmitter.

2.1.3 The wheelhouse manoeuvring and the ECR manoeuvring shall include the following functions :

- Changing the running direction

- Starting and stopping

- Speed regulating

- Emergency stopping

In addition to the above, the wheelhouse manoeuvring shall include programmed speed‑up/speed-

down control with the automatic evasion to avoid running at the barred speed.

For shaft generator(2.1.4)

2.1.4 ME rpm holding function shall be provided from ME remote control system (or ME ECS) for the load shifting to the diesel generator(s), and shall be activated at the following conditions : - Automatic slow down

- Crash astern

- Gradual speed reduction

- Others recommended by the shaft generator Supplier & PMS Supplier

2.1.5 The main engine shall be capable of being manoeuvred at the engine side in the event of the remote

manoeuvring system failure.

2.1.6 The necessary instruments, gauges and an engine telegraph receiver shall be provided at ME side.

2.1.7 ME revolution shall be regulated by the electronic engine control unit.

2.1.8 The engine side manoeuvring shall be actuated (For ME type engine: by the push buttons on the

local operation panel.) manually and mechanically by use of emergency manoeuvring handle and a

push button.

2.1.9 The main engine shall be capable of being started, stopped, reversed, and the engine speed shall be

controlled by the operation of manoeuvring handle.

Restarting shall be carried out after setting the manoeuvring handle on the control stand at “STOP"

position.

2.1.10 The main engine revolution shall be regulated by the (For ME type engine: electronic engine control

unit.) governor actuator receiving the signal of scavenging air pressure and rpm pick up through the

governor control unit.

2.1.12 Slow turning function shall be provided.

2.1.13 In case of more than one (1) set of engine are applied, synchronization function between each

engine’s telegraph shall be provided.

2.2 Shut-down system

2.2.1 The main engine shall be tripped automatically in the following conditions :

- Overspeed

- LO to main engine, low-low pressure

- Thrust pad, high-high temperature

- Engine control system (ECS) shutdown

2.2.2 The reset of shutdown system shall be performed manually. Reset of emergency trip shall not be possible even if the shut-down conditions have been recovered to normal condition, unless the maneuvering lever is placed at the stop position.

2.2.3 Em’cy stop switch shall be provided in the ECR, the wheelhouse and at main engine side.

All shutdown conditions shall be indicated on the ECR console.

2.3 Automatic slow-down

2.3.1 The main engine speed shall be automatically reduced to the recommended speed according to

Supplier's standard in the following conditions :

- ME LO inlet, low pressure

- Thrust pad, high temperature

- Oil mist in crankcase, high

- Jacket cooling water inlet, low pressure

- Jacket cooling water outlet, high temperature after each cylinder

- Piston coolant outlet, high temperature after each cylinder

- Piston coolant outlet, low flow after each cylinder

- Scavenging air each cylinder, high temperature (fire)

- Exhaust gas after each cylinder, high temperature

- Exhaust gas after each cylinder, deviation from average

- Engine control system(ECS) slow down

- Shaft bearing temperature, high

- Intermediate shaft bearing temperature, high

The slow down signal shall be received from the IAS.

Others Automatic slow-down conditions as per main engine Supplier’s recommendation & rule requirement shall be informed in design stage.

2.3.2 Push button for slow-down reset shall be provided on the ECR console.

2.3.3 The slow-down signal shall be fed from the engine room alarm and monitoring system.

All slow-down conditions shall be indicated on the ECR console.

2.4 Emergency run

2.4.1 Even if the main engine is automatically stopped due to automatic shut-down conditions except over

speed and low pressure of main LO inlet, the main engine can be kept running by operating the

canceling push button in wheelhouse, ECR and engine side.

2.4.2 Automatic slow-down canceling push button shall be provided in wheelhouse and canceling push

buttons in ECR.

2.5 Auxiliary blower control

2.5.1 The auxiliary blowers shall be started and stopped automatically by detecting the scavenging air pressure and shall also be remotely started and stopped from the main operation panel (MOP) on the ECR console

2.6 Changeover of control position

The system shall be arranged to operate in the following orders of priority:

Priority

Control position

1st

Local

2nd

ECR

3rd

Wheelhouse or bridge wings

Control position that has higher priority shall be able to take control from another position in lower priority at any time.

The transfer between the differential control positions shall be as follows:

2.6.1 Local - Engine control room

Manoeuvring position changeover between the engine side and the ECR shall be carried out at the

engine side.

2.6.2 Engine control room - Wheelhouse

Manoeuvring position changeover between the wheelhouse and the engine control room shall be

carried out in the ECR.

2.6.3 Wheelhouse – Bridge wing

Manoeuvring position changeover between the wheelhouse and the bridge wing shall be carried out

in the wheelhouse.

2.6.4 Change-over confirmation system shall be applied as per the requirements of the Classification Society and the manufacturer's standard.

2.7 Construction & Materials

2.7.1 Screened cables shall be used for connecting electronic equipment, if necessary, in order to suppress

interference from other equipment.

2.7.2 All components shall be firmly secured so that connecting wires and soldered connections shall not

be mechanically damaged from the vessel’s vibration and shock.

2.7.3 Plug-in connections shall be made of non-rusting materials and shall be effectively locked to ensure

good contact.

2.8 Paint color

Paint color scheme shall be referred to “General Specifications”.

2.9 Sub-telegraph system

Two(2) sets of lamp‑illuminated sub‑telegraph shall be provided on the telegraph receiver or telegraph transmitter on the consoles and ME side with the following divisions :

- Finished with engine

- Stand-by

- At sea

The sub-telegraph on the wheelhouse console and ECR console shall be of lamp illuminated push button type.

2.10 Engine order telegraph logging printer

Two(2) sets of engine order telegraph logging printer shall be provided in the wheelhouse to record the control station, lever position, reply and order time.

Engine order telegraph logging printer shall be interfaced with the master clock.

2.11 The power failure alarm of engine order telegraph system shall be given to wheelhouse and IAS

2.12 A Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) for ME remote control system

(bridge maneuvering system) Shall be carried out by the supplier and the results shall be submitted to the buyer.
