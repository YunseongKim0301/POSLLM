2.3Ventilating system

2.3.1 The supply air to be conditioned by the air handling unit (AHU), and to be delivered to the

2.3.2 The air in airconditioning spaces to be exhausted into corridor and private toilet through the door

louver and door under cut.

2.3.3 The amount of return air through the door louver and door under cut from a conditioned room to corridor to be max. 250 m³/h, and the remained air not returned to be exhausted to weather

directly. In case of the public spaces, the remained air shall be exhausted to weather by the

commonly used exhaust fan for change room, inside locker, etc.

2.3.4 The air in corridor shall be exhausted naturally to weather or shall be re-circulated through stairway

trunk to the air handling unit (AHU) or shall be supplied to the mechanically exhausted spaces

such as sanitary spaces, public toilet, etc.

2.3.5 When the amount of air in corridor is insufficient to supply to the mechanically exhausted spaces, the conditioned air from AHU to be supplied for air balance.

2.3.6 When the amount of return air in Upper deck corridor is insufficient, air-conditioned air (abt. 150 m3/h) to be supplied to each end of upper deck corridor(i.e. on the ceiling near the corridor entrance door from outside) from AHU.

2.3.7 The air in hospital and spot airconditioning spaces except wheelhouse shall not be re-circulated but

to be exhausted naturally or mechanically to weather.

2.3.8 Air outlet of nozzle type to be fitted on the ceiling of wheelhouse windows within the field of

vision.

The hot air heated by duct heater to be used for defrosting in compliance with the ISO 8863 – "Ship's wheelhouse windows heating by hot air of glass panes".

2.3.9 To prevent the entry of cargo vapor gas to the accommodation spaces, the fixed gas detection system to be fitted on fresh air intake of air-con. system. In the event of cargo vapor gas detected, the air-conditioning system to be operated with full re-circulation mode without consideration of design condition in this Section based on the following ;

- All openings exposed to weather to be closed manually.

- All exhaust fans and supply fans in accommodation, except the fans which are operated for full

recirculation mode, to be manually stopped at their starter panel.

- Air in all air-conditioned and spot air-conditioned spaces, except those in the following spaces

provided with shut-off air dampers, to be re-circulated.

- Shut-off air dampers to be provided for hospital and dry provisions store and to be closed

manually by local handles (i.e. no air supply to those spaces).

- The air-conditioning unit for galley to be operated by using the return air from galley.

- CO2 high alarm to be provided in CCR when the CO2 level exceeds 1,200 ppm.

- The details of air balancing work such as closing of air intakes, adjustment of dampers, fan

controls, etc. to be mentioned in the Schematic Diagram of HVAC for Accommodation.

2.1.2.4 Network operation monitoring :

To monitor and detect cyber threats or abnormalities described in UR E26 4.3.1,

cyber threat information with equipment/system(IDS, Firewall, etc.) shall be sent to

shipbuilder’s cyber security equipment by following,

1) Syslog format referred by IETF RFC 5424 (specific format to can be discussed.)

2) IP address to communicate with shipbuilder’s cyber security equipment

3) Logical interface of switch and the firewall to monitor the network operation

(The logical interface (not physical) is required between the network switch, firewall and SecuAider.)

4) Monitoring interface of the managed switch to monitor the network operation

(The mirroring function and the physical interface is required.)

2.1.2.5 Incident response plan :

If the equipment certified by UR E27, supplier shall provide the document for recovery.

If not, the incident response plan shall, as a minimum, include the following information

1) Breakpoints for the isolation of compromised systems

2) A Description of alarms and indicators signaling detected ongoing cyber threat or abnormal behavior

caused by the cyber threat. (threat name, violated firewall rule name, etc.)

3) A Description of expected significant consequences related to cyber incidents (Integrity violation,

Availability violation, Confidentiality violation, etc.)

4) Response options, prioritizing those that do not rely on either shut down or transfer to independent

or local control, if any.

5) Independent and local control information for operating independently from the system that failed

due to the cyber incident, as applicable

2.1.2.6 Recovery plan :

If the equipment certified by UR E27, supplier shall provide the document for recover.

If not, recovery plan shall, as a minimum, include the following information:

1) Instructions and procedures for restoring the failed system without disrupting the operation

from the redundant, independent or local operation.

2) Processes and procedures for the backup and secure storage of information.

3) Current configuration information for all components.
