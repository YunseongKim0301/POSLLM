2. Particulars, Material and Construction

2.1 Equipment general

As shown on Fig.1, heat recovery unit (DCHE), J-T valve(s)and gas/liquid separator shall be installed in a common base frame.Special attention such as thermal insulation and leakage shall be provided as per the Buyer’s requirements and Class requirements or recommendations.

The BOG generation from LNG stored in the cargo tanks is inevitable due to heat ingress into the cargo tanks, even though the cargo tanks are well insulated. Therefore, the BOG has to be removed in order to maintain equilibrium within the cargo tanks within the target operating pressure.

The BOG from cargo tanks is evacuated by the HP FG compressor. The BOG is compressed up to approximately 305 bar g. through the HP FG compressor. The compressed BOG is entered into the MEGI main engines as FG and the surplus BOG is entered into the counter-current Exchanger (DCHE).

After passing through the DCHE, J-T valve(s)and gas/liquid separator, the BOG is liquefied as either saturated LNG or sub-cooled LNG.

From the gas/liquid separator, the saturated LNG(re-liquefied LNG) is returned back to cargo tanks via a liquid control valve and separate condensate main line.

The operational system pressure of BOG shall be of from approx. 306 bar a.

Anti-freezing device for cryogenic heat exchanger shall be provided. A gas detection system for insulation space shall be provided.

Appropriate filter (fine filter) shall be arranged on all feed lines of heat exchangers such as DCHE soas to protect the channels from blocking.

Others such as constructions, materials, etc., of the full reliquefaction system shall be in accordance with the Manufacturer's standards and the requirements of the Classification Society, the Rules and Regulations.

Skid fabrication shall be done in clean space and protection for dust ingress shall be provided until onboard fabrication.
